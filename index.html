02 Executive Summary

The Opportunity at a Glance

  • Reposition ~98.8 acres of underutilized, EDC-owned business park land into a high-quality semi-custom single-family community
  • 150–175 lots on 0.3–0.5 acre parcels with an average selling price in the low $400s
  • The business park has remained vacant with approximately $7M in sunk public cost—this converts a stranded asset into durable tax base and utility customers
  • Creates $60–75M in new taxable value, producing approximately $265k/year in recurring City revenue
  • Strengthens employer recruitment by providing a modern housing ladder for managers, engineers, and skilled trades relocating to the area
Core thesis

Convert a cost center into a revenue engine while addressing Lampasas' undersupply of modern, semi-custom residential product on larger lots.

11 Developer Thesis

Why a Developer Should Pursue This

  • EDC-owned land with negotiable pricing and a motivated public-sector counterparty
  • Chapter 380 agreements, land grants, infrastructure grants, and tax abatements available
  • Municipal utilities already proximate — a major de-risk versus typical rural Central Texas subdivisions
  • City leaders publicly discussing selling and appraising Lot 4 — a clear political signal
  • 0.3–0.5 acre lots create a differentiated product with limited direct competition in Lampasas proper
  • Low land basis relative to Burnet County comps; transportation catalysts de-risk the multi-phase hold
Key structural advantage

This is one of the only large, infrastructure-served residential development sites in Lampasas County with a motivated, single public-entity seller and an established incentive toolkit.

13 Execution Strategy

Entitlement Path & Design Framework

Zoning & Entitlement

  • Current: BPZ2 / BPZ3 (Business Park). Residential requires rezoning.
  • Recommended path: PUD overlay or targeted carve-out with design standards
  • “Mixed edge” strategy: employment-flex pads near park spine; residential deeper along CR 4006

Design Standards

  • Stone / hardie / masonry elevation requirements
  • Roof pitch minimums & dark-sky lighting
  • Trail / greenway along natural drainage corridors
  • Buffers & deed restrictions to business park

Deal Structure

  • Performance-based public–private agreement
  • Milestones: plat, vertical starts, min. assessed value
